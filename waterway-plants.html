Clustered, Slender Wallaby-Grass - Rytidosperma racemosum

Small, graceful species with fine folage and slender seedheads. Able to persist in very weedy areas.

Drooping Sheoak - Allocasuarina verticillata

Small erect tree with a dense rounded crown and drooping grey-green branches. Bark fissured. Prefers well drained soils and full sun. Habitat for birds and caterpillars.

Fragrant Saltbush - Rhagodia parabolica

Low-growing, spreading shrub with grey-green leaves. Clusters of red berries in late spring and summer. Prefers well drained soils. Drought tolerant. Catterpillar friendly.

Grey Tussock-Grass - Poa sieberiana

Forms a small tussock, with fine grey-green leaves. Flowerheads are green or purplish. Ideal for rockeries and under eucalypts. Habitat for butterlies.

Kangaroo Grass - Themeda triandra

Forms a large tussock. Foliage is purple-green in spring, then turns a rusty colour in summer. Flower spikes with rusty seed heads in summer. Prefers well drained soil. Butterfly friendly.

Nodding Saltbush - Einadia nutans

A perennial groundcover with small silver-green arrow-shaped leaves. Can cover other rocks and plants. Many small red and yellow berries in small clusters. Provides food for small reptiles and birds. Prefers dry, well-drained soil.

Ruby Saltbush - Enchylaena tomentosa

Hardy prostrate shrub with small, succulent, grey-green leaves. Yellow to red berries all year, especially in summer. Prefers sunny position, in well drained soils. Grows well on rocky slopes. Food for blue-tongue lizards

Slender or Rough Spear-Grass - Austrostipa scabra

Fine-leaved, tussock forming grass with many soft, silky flowerheads in spring and summer. Very drought tolerant. Best planted in clumps.

Spiny-Headed Mat-Rush - Lomandra longifolia

A large tussock with bright green, strap like leaves. Prefers moist, well-drained soils. Very hardy and drought tolerant. Suitable for rock gardens.

Weeping Grass - Microlaena stipoides

A semi-prostate grass with dropping flowerheads. Needs moist well drained soils. Able to colonise large areas quickly. Butterfly habitat.
