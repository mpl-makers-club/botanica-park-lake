Chocolate Lily - Arthropodium strictum

An erect perennial lily (60cm) with purple to mauve flowers appearing in spring. Plant has a distinctive chocolate scent. Prefers moist, well-drained soils. Plants respond well to periodic watering. Plants die back to tubers in dry summers, and shoot again in autumn. Effective when planted in groups. Tubers are edible, raw or roasted.

Matted-Flex Lily - Dianella amoena

Clusters of blue flowers on stems in spring and summer. Eventually bears blue berries. Leaves are glue-green with tiny teeth along the edges. Plant is relatively rare.

Nodding Saltbush - Einadia nutans

A perennial groundcover with small silver-green arrow-shaped leaves. Can cover other rocks and plants. Many small red and yellow berries in small clusters. Provides food for small reptiles and birds. Prefers dry, well-drained soil.

Ruby Saltbush - Enchylaena tomentosa

Hardy prostrate shrub with small, succulent, grey-green leaves. Yellow to red berries all year, especially in summer. Prefers sunny position, in well drained soils. Grows well on rocky slopes. Food for blue-tongue lizards.

Running Postman - Kennedia prostrata

An attractive trailing groundcover with soft, green clover-like leaves. Large scarlet-red pea flowers in spring. Prefers well drained, gravelly soils. The flowers are rich in nectar and attractive to honeyeaters. Attractor for other birds and insects. Suitable as a groundcover under trees. Cascades over retaining walls.
