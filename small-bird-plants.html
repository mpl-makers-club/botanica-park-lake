Bushy Needlewood - Hakea decurrens ssp. physocarpa

A straggly, erect prostrate spreading shrub with stiff, narrow pointed leaves. Masses of scented, white or pink flowers in winter and early spring. Birds are attracted to the flowers, woody fruit and prickly growth.

Hairpin Banksia - Banksia spinulosa

Dense erect spreading shrub. Narrow dark green leaves. Flowers form as golden-yellow brushes. Prefers moist well drained soils. Can be propagated from seeds and cuttings. Bird friendly.

Hedge Wattle - Acacia paradoxa

A dense, thorny shrub with small dark green leaves. Large, ornamental golden-yellow ball flowers in spring. Prefers well drained soils. Excellent bird habitat. Thorns help to protect birds and other animals from predators.

Rosemary Grevillea - Grevillea rosmarinifolia

A dense, rounded shrub with narrow, dark green, prickly leaves. Red and cream flowers in winter and spring. Very useful for attracting honeyeaters. A hardy shrub.

Ruby Saltbush - Enchylaena tomentosa

Hardy prostrate shrub with small, succulent, grey-green leaves. Yellow to red berries all year, especially in summer. Prefers sunny position, in well drained soils. Grows well on rocky slopes. Food for blue-tongue lizards.

Spreading Wattle - Acacia genistifolia

Fast growing, open spreading shrub. Clusters of lemon to cream ball shaped flowerheads. Tolerates wet or dry soil. This plant is a good refuge for small birds. May self-seed in the garden.

Tree Violet - Melicytus dentatus

A rigid, grounded shrub with many spines and small leaves. Bears tiny, fragrant, yellow, bell-shaped flowers in spring followed by cream to purple berries in summer. The berries are an important food source for Cunningham's Skinks. A very hardy, slow growing shrub. Responds well to pruning.
