Spear-grasses

Soft Spear-Grass - Austrostipa mollis

Forms a small, erect tussock with large, dense flower heads on robust stems in spring and summer. Very drought tolerant. Often occurs naturally in large drifts.

Slender Spear-Grass - Austrostipa scabra ssp. falcata

Am erect, fine leaved, tussock-forming grass with numerous soft, silky flowerheads in spring and summer. Very drought tolerant. Best planted in groups.

Other native grasses

Common Tussock-Grass - Poa labillardierei

Forms a large tussock. Long green, occasionally blue-green leaves that dry to a straw colour. Plume like flowerheads in spring and summer. A fast growing tussock that tolerates a wide range of conditions. Prefers an open position in moist, well-drained soils. Old tussocks can be reguveniated by pruning and by adding extra water. Bird and butterfly friendly. Suitable for rockeries and beside ponds.

Grey Tussock-Grass - Poa sieberiana

Forms a small tussock, with fine grey-green leaves. Loose green or purplish flowerheads are held above the foliage. Prefers dry, well drained soils. Ideal for rockeries or under eucalypts.

Weeping Grass - Microlaena stipoides

A slender perrenial, semi-prostrate grass with dropping flowerheads. Prefers a sheltered position in moist, well drained soils. Able to colonise large areas quickly.

Wallaby-Grasses

Bristly Wallaby-Grass - Rytidosperma setaceum var. setaceum

Fine foliage and small seedheads on erect stems. Very common in grasslands. Plant height 30-40cm. Stems 60cm long.

Clustered Wallaby-Grass - Rytidosperma racemosum

A small graceful species with fine foliage and slender seedheads. A hardy grass in mixed grasslands. Persists in very weedy areas.

Copper-Awned Wallaby-Grass - Rytidosperma fulvum

Native grass up to 40cm height. Attractive white fluffy seedheads up to 70cm. Very drought tolerant. Bird attracting.

Kneed Wallaby-Grass - Rytidosperma geniculatum

A very decorative small tussock with fine green leaves and dense, compact growth. Numerous buff-coloured flowerheads in spring and summer. Suitable for rockeries. Butterfly friendly.
