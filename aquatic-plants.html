Common Reed - Phragmites australia

Tall reed 3m. Very common in waterways. Dense habitat for birds and frogs.

Common Sedge - Carex tereticaulis

Tall sedge that forms stiff, cylindrical stems. Brown spikes in spring. Important to creek edges.

Eel Grass - Vallisneria spp.

Grassy plants in shallow to deep permanent water, forming thickets that reach to the surface. Propagated by division and spreads quickly by runners. Often seen in aquariums.

Flecked Flat-Sedge - Cyperus gunnii

Tufted sedge with grass-like leaves. Reddy-brown flower heads. Prefers swampy areas.

Marsh Club-Rush or Slat Club-Sedge - Bolboschoenus caldwellii

Lime-green triangular stems and small, golden flowering spikes in Spring. Spreads via underground stems. Birds use stems for cover and nesting material.

Narrow-Leaf Cumbungi or Bullrush - Typha domingensis

Tall reed with large, bullrush flower spikes. A significant Koorie plant with many uses.

Slender Knotweed - Persicaria decipiens

Vigorous plant. Long green leaves with black or brown blotches. Similar to Vietnamese mint in appearance. In summer and autumn stems have attractive small pink flower spikes.

Tall Sedge - Carex appressa

Rounded, dome shaped tussock. Sharply serrated leaf edges. Prefer permanent moisture. Leaves used by Aborigines in basket weaving.

Tall Spike-Rush - Eleocharis sphacelata

Tall, leafless, spike-like rush. Stems thick but hollow. Tall (1-2m) above water and tolerates deep, still water. Small flowerhead at the tip of the spike. Can spread vigorously. Dense habitat for birds and frogs.

Tassel Sedge - Carex fascicularis

Dense tussock with bright green foliage. Flowers dangle from slender stems. Prefers wet soils.

Tufted Sedge - Carex gaudichaudiana

Grows in scattered clumps. Prefers moist soils. Green-blue foliage.

Water Ribbons - Triglochin procera

An attractive plants with long leaves that float on top of the water. The plant oxygenates the water which helps fish breath. In spring and summer flower spikes with white flowers emerge from the water.
